Ready to Start?

Here's how to take your first real step

📋 Your Pre-Flight Checklist

Before investing a single dollar, make sure you have these basics covered:

Step 1: Emergency Fund

Save 3-6 months of expenses in a high-yield savings account. This is your safety net — don't invest until you have this. Think of it like having a full health bar before going into a boss fight.

Step 2: Kill High-Interest Debt

If you have credit card debt (usually 20%+ interest), pay that off first. No investment consistently beats 20% returns, so paying off debt IS your best investment.

Step 3: Start Small

You don't need thousands to start. Many platforms let you begin with $1. The goal isn't the amount — it's building the habit and starting early so compound interest works for you.

🛠️ Beginner-Friendly Platforms

📱

Robinhood

Great for beginners. No minimum, fractional shares, simple interface. Start with an index fund like VOO (S&P 500) or VTI (total market).

🏦

Fidelity

Zero-fee index funds, great research tools, and excellent customer service. Perfect for opening a Roth IRA (your future self will thank you).

🏛️

Vanguard

The OG of index fund investing. Founded by Jack Bogle himself. Low fees, investor-owned, built for long-term wealth building.

💳

High-Yield Savings

Before investing, park your emergency fund in a HYSA (like Marcus, Ally, or SoFi). Earns 4-5% vs the 0.01% at most big banks.

🎯 The Simplest Possible Plan

If all of this still feels overwhelming, here is literally all you need to do:

1. Open a Roth IRA at Fidelity or Vanguard
2. Set up automatic monthly deposits (even $25/month)
3. Buy a total market index fund (like VTI or FZROX)
4. Don't touch it. Let it grow. Check it once a quarter max.

That's it. Seriously. You'll outperform most professional investors over time.
